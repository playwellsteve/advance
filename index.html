Welcome to Play-Well's 2017 Fall Advance

Being an Area Manager can sometimes feel like a lonely job. The good news is: it doesn’t have to be. This year, Play-Well managers get the chance to come together, share ideas, broaden their knowledge base, and learn from each other during the Fall Retreat. 

This year we are returning to the beautiful San Francisco Theological Seminary in San Anselmo, CA

This website is your go-to resource for up-to-date information about the Retreat. You can find the agenda for each day, summaries of the workshops our management team has prepared for you, and pertinent information for your travel days. Feel free to have a look around, and if you have any questions, please ask your Regional or District Manager.

See you on October 9th!

Workshops

Mandatory Workshops

Tim Bowen

Welcome and Financial Overview

Introduced to the LEGO® building system in 1996 and recognizing it to be both a popular play medium and a tremendous learning medium, he began to develop projects which drew on his professional experience and combined the elements of fun, challenge, and learning. As we celebrate 20 years of teaching engineering to kids, Tim loves interacting with the people who make Play Well what it is!

Jeff Harry & Brie Laffey

Play-Well Why & Values In Action Workshop

In the grind of the day-to-day, we sometimes forget about the big picture, the reason why we do what we do. This workshop is to remind us of Play-Well’s why and the values that has contributed to the organization’s success over the past 20 years.

Alex Shullman & Ben Pfister

Curriculum / Training: Consistency, Communication, and Feedback

We’ll be focused how the training team can work together with managers and veteran instructors to improve the consistency of the Engineering Core training experience. The main component of this will be to discuss and make a plan as to how each manager will go through the Engineering Core Training over the next 6 months.

Amy Good

STEM Exploration and STEAM Academy

Plunge into daytime programs and explore two different variations of shorter STEM programs designed to pitch to schools and districts during regular school hours. You will build, price out, look for potential clients, and even get an opportunity to test it out at Davidson Middle School later this week. Bring your laptops, your questions, and your enthusiasm to take a risk and try these programs in your area(s).

Lisa Doering

Full Day Camps - Let's Get to Yes!

We have talked about developing full day camp programs for years, and wondered how to solve the many questions around this. In this hands-on work group session, you’ll hear success stories from managers who have tried these, suggestions on how you can start adding full day programs to your seasonal camp schedules, and answers to frequently asked questions. Instead of letting ourselves get blocked by the challenges, we’ll figure out how to get to YES! Each of you will work with your regional manager to choose viable clients to approach with this idea, set pricing and goals for each season, and even reach out to a client to see if you can get THEM to yes!

Optional Workshops

Brie Laffey

Managers' Showcase

A variety of managers will be presenting short segments of teachings and learnings from their individual areas and how they can help you.  We will be learning from other managers in a fast paced setting that will spark future conversations!

Steve Halford

Exploring Data to Tame Risk

Intelligent risk taking and good decision making depend to a significant degree on analyzing the past. There is a tendency, though, to look at the same data in the same way. In this workshop, we want to explore new ways of looking at our data and examine new sources of data that can aid in our analysis.  Together we’ll come up with some questions that we can ask of our data and explore ways in which we look at our data to better understand these questions.  We’ll also look at connecting our course and enrollment data to outside data to expand the range of questions that we can pose. The goal is to give you a better idea of how the analysis process works, what sort of data we have available, and how we go about posing questions that we can address with data.

Bob Lund

Excel 101

If “excel” seems like a dirty word, then this workshop is for you. Join us as we’ll walk through some of the most common spreadsheet tasks. Participants will learn how to create simple formulas, apply filters, sort results and use conditional formatting to create a list of all of the schools in their area. Bonus—no memorization needed!

Alex Pearce & Maddy Gabor

PW Pricing Palooza

Let's dig into pricing together! As we grow and develop new programs to meet the needs of a changing market, we sometimes face challenging pricing situations. How do we know if we are breaking even? How can we make it work with fewer students or a community that has fewer resources? In this workshop, we will run some exercises to breakdown how we can price different programs in different areas and what we can do to meet the evolving needs of our clients.

Erin Morse & Joe Roberts

Night At the Improv

Come Join us for a laugh-filled workshop where anyone can join in the hi-jinx, or just sit back and laugh (jeers from the audience are highly encouraged)! This workshop will consist of  interactive games and discussions to help conquer your fears of public speaking and/or develop your thinking on the fly.

Maddy Gabor

SWOT Analysis

In this workshop, we will take a look at the internal and external forces that affect our areas. Through analyzing strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats we can develop strong strategies for future seasons. In particular we will focus on action items that help us leverage our strengths, take advantage of opportunities while also minimizing weaknesses and threats.

Maddy Gabor & Mary Kate Boyd

Judgment and Decision Making in Tough Times

Something bad has happened. Your client is really mad, a parent has been yelling at you, someone is threatening to sue you and your instructor is crying—it’s not a great day. What do you do?   As managers, we have a lot of power to limit fallout when something goes wrong. Join us for a workshop dedicated to dealing with fires in the best way possible. Practice de-escalating, and learn tools, language and procedures that can help guide you when dealing with a fire in your area.

Erik Olson

Dead (and living) Poets Society

O captain, my captain!  Come sound your barbaric YAWP as we share our favorite poems.  Bring a famous one, a not-so-famous one, a poem you wrote yourself--totally up to you!

Austin Lazek

Better Stories, Better Classes

We all have stories to tell, but how do we bring these to the classroom in an interesting way?  Join CO area manager Austin Lazek in discussing what makes stories "compelling," ways to make scenarios and challenges more dynamic, and how to encourage our instructors to implement these elements to create a more powerful and memorable classroom experience.  Participants will be telling stories of their own and working together to create wacky scenarios.  Hope to see you there!

Alice Ostrovsky & Aaron Shanahan

Hike at Samuel P. Taylor Park

Get out and stretch your legs! We'll take a short drive to visit Samuel P Taylor State Park, home of the majestic coastal redwoods. There are a ton of hiking trails, ranging from partially paved to dirt in the 1-3 mile range. More adventurous folks can hike to the top of Barnabe peak (a 5.8 mi loop). The rest of us can hug some trees, stroll through the forest, explore fairy circles or just sit on a rock by the creek and decompress before heading back to the Seminary for dinner.

Lauren Yee & Jeff Harry

GSD and Freeing Up Your Day

Tedious and mundane tasks can feel like they're taking over your day.  Figure out how to get to the work that matters!  Identify and eliminate redundancies, so you can feel less overwhelmed and spend more of each day getting (the important) stuff done.

Alex Pearce

Email Etiquette and internet usage

As we all know, a large part of our job involves sending and receiving emails. Whether receiving parent feedback or confirming camp descriptions and details, we depend on our ability to communicate both clearly and concisely via email. In this workshop, we will dig into some useful tips and tricks for how to structure emails, reduce client confusion, and of course, share some funny (and benign) email anecdotes. Come prepared to share, craft, and maybe even laugh.

San Francisco Theological Seminary

Welcome to the Jungle or Finding your way around the San Francisco Theological Seminary

Our home for this year’s retreat is the San Francisco Theological Seminary ("SFTS"). Since it’s a new place for many of us, here’s some info about it:

Address: 105 Seminary Rd, San Anselmo, CA 94960 Phone #: (415) 451-2800.

Locations: We are staying in Baird Hall. Please look to the postings at the site for specific room assignments. Most of our meetings will be held in Lower Alexander Hall, which is a short walk from Baird (maybe 100 yds?). Breakfast and dinner will mostly be served in Upper Alexander Hall (upstairs from our meeting room) with lunches varying from day to day. We will be walking to Play Well Marin (approx 1 mi away) twice during the week. Please let your RM know if you'll need a ride instead.

Parking: Please obey all posted street signs. Overnight street parking is not allowed in San Anselmo so we will need to make sure our cars are parked in the nearby SFTS lot.

Housing: If you are staying on site, please be aware that the SFTS is not a fancy hotel. While certainly clean and adequate, the accommodations are nothing spectacular. Be advised that bathroom facilities are also somewhat shared-- there are 10 private shower rooms as well as men's and women's bathrooms on each of the two floors. All bedding and towels are provided.

Getting to the San Rafael Transit Center

Upon arrival in the Bay Area, you will be taking one of two shuttles to the San Rafael Transit Center, based on which airport you flew into. Both are nice charter-coach type busses and come equipped with wifi and an onboard bathroom. For both shuttles, the drivers will assist with your bags and will offer to have a cab waiting for you (which you do not need as we will come pick you up from there). If they ask for your destination, you are heading to the San Rafael Transit Center. Both trips generally take about an hour.

San Francisco (SFO)

The Marin Airporter offers service every 30 minutes. From their website:

Buses pick up at SFO every 30 minutes, on approximately the hour and half-hour, beginning at 5:00 AM. The last bus of the night departs from SFO at midnight. No reservations are required...

Pick up locations for all domestic flights are outside of baggage claim (located on the lower level), on the center courtesy island, at the green and white curb. Look for the sign that says "Airporters".

From the International Terminal, meet the bus on the arrivals/baggage claim level, on the center island, at the green and white striped curb. Look for the sign saying "Airporters".

Please Note: Airport regulations require that buses pick up passengers ONLY at our designated pickup points. Our drivers are not allowed to stop for passengers flagging them down from any other zone.

Cost: The fare is $22 and must be paid in cash. They put this in big, bold, red letters on their website: ALL FARES ARE CASH ONLY!

Oakland (OAK)
(VAN)

We will have a list of suggested departure times posted at Baird Hall. Please be sure that you are packed and ready to go 15 minutes before we need to leave the Seminary to help us stay on schedule. We also recommend that you check in to your return flight either on-line or with your airlines mobile app to have a smooth a trip as possible.

As with the arrivals, you will be taking a different shuttle based on which airport you are flying out of. Unless you have different airpots for the different legs of your trip, it should be the same shuttle company. As before, you will need to either have cash (for SFO or OAK flights) or pre-purchase your fare (OAK only).
In addition to the standard stuff (socks, toiletries, laptop, chargers, and the like) please note that Northern California is not all beaches and sunshine. While we might have temps in the 80s it is also very possible that they'll dip into the 40s. It is almost a certainty that we'll have fog in the mornings and it is quite likely that we'll get a bit of rain as well. In short, pack for everything short of snow (::knocks on wood::)
